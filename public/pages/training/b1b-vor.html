VOR Approach Guide

Non-precision VOR/DME approach for the B-1B. Uses a ground-based VOR station for lateral guidance. Step-down altitudes to MDA — no automatic glideslope. Example: VOR/DME RWY 34 at KDYS using ABILENE VORTAC (ABI) 113.70.

Phase 2 — FMS setup for VOR approach
1
FMS NAV page — tune VOR
FMS → NAV NAV1 [113.70] → --->VORTAC [ABI] CRS [345] → ← inbound course from plate NAV MODE [NAV] → ← NAV, not GPS
  • Type 113.70 in scratch pad → line-select into NAV1
  • Confirm ident shows VORTAC — ABI
  • Set CRS to the inbound course from the approach plate
  • NAV MODE = NAV so NAV HOLD tracks the VOR radial
  • PFD softkey = NAV1
2
FMS PERF → APPR — set MDA
FMS → PERF → APPR PG FLAPS --> 17° VREF --> □□□ Kt MDA/H --> □□□□ ← from VOR/DME plate LAND QNH --> 29.92
  • Enter MDA from the VOR/DME approach plate
  • MDA is typically higher than an ILS DH — expect reduced ceiling margins
  • Update QNH from ATIS (KDYS ATIS: 269.175)

!
Step-down fixes and DME
  • VOR/DME approaches use DME distance to define each step-down fix
  • Read the DME readout from the NAV display — cross-check against the plate
  • Do not descend below a step-down altitude until past that DME fix
  • Final step down is to MDA — engage ALT HOLD at MDA
!
VOR station passage
  • If the VOR is on the field (overhead): expect a TO/FROM flag flip as you cross the station
  • Brief station passage in the approach brief — deviation needle will swing
  • NAV HOLD may briefly destabilize at station passage — normal
  • ABI is 3.8 NM from the field — you will pass close but not overhead
