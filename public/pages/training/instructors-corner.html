Common Student Errors
  • ILS: Wrong PFD softkey (GPS instead of ILS). APR not armed before LOC alive.
  • NAV MODE: Left on GPS instead of switching to NAV for ILS approach.
  • RNAV: Pressing APR instead of NAV HOLD. Forgetting ALT HOLD at MDA.
  • ATH: Relying on autothrottle below 2,500 ft AGL — it disengages at that altitude.
  • Go-around: Delayed call. Coach immediate execution at DH with no runway contact.
📋
Preflight Brief Checklist — IP
  • Quiz VR and VREF from FMS PERF page before taxi
  • Confirm student knows pattern altitude (2,500 ft MSL) and circuit direction
  • For ILS/RNAV: student must state the correct PFD softkey and A/P lateral mode before the brief ends
  • Brief go-around criteria explicitly: engine issues, unstable approach, ATC instruction
  • Phase 3 = observed only — intervention limited to safety of flight
!
Common Student Errors
  • Weapon release out of envelope: below 20,000 ft AGL or 230 KIAS. Have student call out altitude and airspeed before every attempt.
  • Stealth Mode: button has no effect below 3,000 ft AGL — student assumes broken control rather than checking altitude.
  • APP mode won't capture: FMS NAV MODE set to GPS instead of NAV. First check, every time.
  • Cargo doors left open after release: unnecessary drag on egress. Make closure a verbal callout item.
  • Wrong sim version: confirm iniBuilds store version before briefing any weapon delivery event.
📋
Preflight Brief Checklist — IP
  • Confirm iniBuilds store version before any weapon delivery event
  • Quiz the 3 release criteria (20,000 ft / 230 KIAS / wings level)
  • Brief CFD flight model differences — handling won't match real Spirit
  • Brief Stealth Mode altitude restriction (3,000 ft AGL minimum)
  • Confirm student can state FMS NAV MODE requirement for APP before stepping to jet

Common Student Errors by Phase
  • Phase 1: Over-rotating on takeoff — the B-52's pitch response is slower than fighter instincts expect. Coach steady, deliberate rotation, not a snap to attitude.
  • Phase 2 (AR): Over-controlling in the contact position. Coach small inputs and an early, decisive breakaway call rather than trying to salvage an unstable picture.
  • Phase 3 (TOT): TOT drift not corrected early enough — by the IP it's usually too late to fix within ±30 sec. Coach continuous time checks against waypoints throughout the low-level, not just near the target.
  • Phase 3 (Night): Exterior/interior lighting not reconfigured before the night portion — make this a deliberate checklist item, not an afterthought.
📋
Preflight Brief Checklist — IP
  • Confirm student has briefed TOLD V-speeds for current gross weight before stepping — heavy unstick varies meaningfully
  • For Phase 2: confirm tanker rejoin type and contact altitude block are briefed explicitly
  • For Phase 3: brief low-level abort criteria (weather, terrain clearance, TOT recovery limits) before the flight
  • Brief safe-escape maneuver and timing before the IP — not the first time on the run-in
